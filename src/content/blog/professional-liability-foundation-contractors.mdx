Foundation repair contractors carry a quiet kind of risk: the risk of being wrong. When your professional recommendation is alleged to have failed, you need professional liability insurance, also called errors and omissions (E&O).

## What Professional Liability Actually Covers
It responds to claims that your professional advice, design, or judgment caused a financial loss — recommending the wrong pier system, specifying an inadequate drainage plan, or misdiagnosing settlement.

## A Real-World Example
You recommend helical piers to a certain depth. The repair is installed flawlessly, but two years later the home settles again because the soil profile was deeper than anticipated. With no bodily injury and no sudden property damage, GL may decline — professional liability defends you.

## Why Foundation Work Specifically Needs It
- You diagnose problems and recommend solutions
- Foundation failures are high-value and attract attorneys
- Soil behavior is uncertain
- Clients increasingly require E&O on contracts
