The most dangerous liability in foundation repair is the kind you do not see coming. Unlike a roof leak that shows up next week, foundation claims often surface months or years after the crew has packed up.

## 1. Soil Movement and Settlement
Expansive clay, poor compaction, and changing moisture can cause a repaired foundation to settle after the work is complete — and the homeowner often points at the last contractor who touched it.

## 2. Adjacent-Property Damage
Excavation, drilling, and underpinning can disturb neighboring structures, creating third-party claims for cracked driveways, shifted walls, and damaged utilities.

## 3. Latent Structural Defects
A repair that looks perfect on completion can develop problems later — a pier that does not reach competent strata, or a stabilization system that underperforms under load.

## 4. Professional Judgment Risk
Every diagnosis and recommendation is professional advice, creating errors-and-omissions exposure that general liability typically excludes.

## 5. Pollution and Environmental Exposure
Drilling fluids and disturbed contaminated soil can trigger environmental claims that the standard GL pollution exclusion leaves uncovered.
